We Are Partners for Forest

Committed to developing legacy forests, fostering ecological resilience, and building strong partnerships.

Our Roots & History

Fostering People's Education, Empowerment and Enterprise, Inc. (FosPEEE) is a non-profit, non-stock, non-government organization that aims to promote community empowerment for environmental stewardship, restoration, and conservation of biodiversity, and sustainable development in key conservation sites in the Philippines.

It was established in January 2004 and formally registered at the Securities and Exchange Commission on April 15, 2004. FosPEEE’s operations have been focused on the Upper Marikina Watershed, particularly in the Tayabasan and Boso-Boso sub-watersheds in partnership with 11 People’s Organizations based in Sitio Apia, Barangay Calawis, and Sitio San Ysiro in Barangay San Jose, Antipolo City, province of Rizal. But in 2025, its operations expanded to the Ipo Watershed in Norzagaray, Bulacan.

Our Guiding Principles

Purpose

To restore and protect native Philippine forest ecosystems through ecological science and human collaboration, ensuring biodiversity and sustainable resources for future generations.

Mission

To implement, advocate, and scale the Rainforestation farming system in degraded landscapes, creating self-sustaining **Legacy Forests** in partnership with local communities, government, and the private sector.

Vision

A Philippines where thriving, biodiverse forest ecosystems are valued and sustainably managed by empowered communities, contributing to national resilience against climate change.

Core Strategies: How We Work

1. Ecological Innovation

Pioneering and refining the Rainforestation method, using only native species suited to the specific microclimates of the region.

2. Partnership Building

Fostering enduring, collaborative relationships with all stakeholders, including landowners, government agencies, and indigenous peoples, to ensure shared ownership and long-term success.

3. Community Empowerment

Providing training and resources to local communities, enabling them to become effective forest stewards and derive sustainable livelihoods.

4. Policy Advocacy

Working to influence environmental policy to better recognize and support native species conservation and sustainable forestry practices.
